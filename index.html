I Like Music

Still Life

Dawn Golden

When I found out about Still Life shortly after it came out, I listened to it on repeat for about a week. I still listen to it on repeat sometimes. Sometimes the lyrics can be contrived, but it works as a whole. There's also a remix album if you want more bass and have a sad party with friends (or on your own).

Lighght

Kishi Bashi

This is one of the most magical albums you will ever hear. K writes about everlasting love in fairytale settings (just read the lyrics for Bittersweet Genesis for Him AND Her), while the instrumentals transport you to a foreign pixie wonderland.

Lanterns

Son Lux

This is probably my favorite album as a whole. All the tracks stand solidly on their own while working together to paint the same larger picture. The last track, Lanterns Lit, is hauntingly beautiful and even better live!

White Lighter

Typhoon

Admittedly, this isn't something you just put on and get on with the rest of your day, unless you're okay with getting dirty looks from singing along. The lyrics can be a little strange, e.g. One Hundred Years talks about waking up 100 years later to find that your limbs have been cut off.

Heza

Generationals

These guys make really good pop. It's fun music! Even as a self-proclaimed fun-hater, I can't hate this. It almost makes me want to dance, and that is probably as close to dancing as I'll ever get. They do have a newer album out, called Alix, but it hasn't grown on me yet.

I Like Movies

The Royal Tenenbaums

Admittedly, there is not a Wes Anderson movie that I do not like. I also have a huge soft spot for movies that deal extensively with family relations, so it's nearly impossible that I wouldn't like this.

Ghost in the Shell

Like Akira, this is an anime you don't get judged for liking because it was made in the 90s and that somehow makes it (and you by extension) cool. The animation is fantastic and Motoko is perhaps one of the most badass female characters ever.

The Usual Suspects

I should've watched this much sooner, before I spoiled it for myself (thanks, Key and Peele). Even though I had a pretty good idea of how it would end, it was still thoroughly enjoyable. The cast is stellar, and I acquired a newfound respect for Kevin Spacey after watching this.

All About Lily Chou‑Chou

This was a little hard to watch, but my appreciation for it grew as time went on. The build-up is incredibly slow but rewarding, and the heavy atmosphere is maintained throughout. Fourteen-year-olds lead tough lives, really.

Melancholia

Surprisingly, I liked the first half better, which focuses on the sister most people hated. Perhaps I'm a bad judge of character. This movie is about two sisters who are a little off in the head, albeit in completely different ways.
